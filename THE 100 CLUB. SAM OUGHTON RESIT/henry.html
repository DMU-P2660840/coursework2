    • Thierry Daniel Henry is a retired French professional football player and one of the greatest players in the history of the English Premier League. This small-town footballer grew to be an all-time leading international goal scorer for club Arsenal and the France National Team. With 175 goals in the Premier League, Henry ranks seventh in the League's history as of today. He is currently a French football coach and assistant coach for the Belgium National Team until December 2022, having just played in the Qatar World Cup. During his eight-year stint with the Gunners starting in 1999, he scored an unmatched 226 goals. He was named the PFA Players' Player of the Year twice, and the PFA Team of the Year six consecutive times. In 2003, Henry was runner-up for the Ballon d'Or, and in 2003-2004, he was runner-up for the FIFA World Player of the Year award. Henry joined FC Barcelona in 2007 for a $20.8 million fee (£16 million). He was a part of Barcelona under the then manager, Pep Guardiola, in 2009, winning six record trophies. It includes the EUFA Champions League, Spain’s major domestic cup (Copa del Rey), LaLiga, Spanish Supercup, the UEFA Supercup, and the Club World Cup. He chose to retire from international football after the FIFA World Cup in 2010. Post his release from Barca in 2010, Henry signed with the Major League Soccer side, the New York Red Bulls. On December 16, after a four-and-a-half-year stint with the club, he announced his retirement as a football player. Post his retirement, he worked as a pundit for Sky Sports for over three years and quit his position in July 2018 to become a coach.Henry finished second in the Ballon d’Or voting in 2003. He was named FIFA World Player of the Year in 2004. He was named PFA Players’ Player of the Year twice. FWA Footballer of the Year three times. PFA Team of the Year six times in a row. Henry was also named to the FIFA FIFPro World XI once and five times to the UEFA Team of the Year. During the 2000s, he was one of the most commercially marketed footballers. Henry, along with Alan Shearer, were inducted into the Premier League Hall of Fame in 2021.
